Why this map exists

Environmental science needs more than warnings.

Students spend years learning what's broken. This map asks a different question: where has it actually been fixed, and how? Every pin is a real place, a real problem, and a real solution, documented by the people studying it.

Grounded in planetary boundaries

Every story is tagged to what it actually addressed.

From climate and biodiversity to novel entities like plastics, each solution is mapped against the boundary it helped pull back from the edge.

How it works

Three steps from field notes to a pin on the map.

A title, up to 100 words on the solution, and the planetary boundaries it addressed.
