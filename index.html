Why this map exists

Environmental science needs more than warnings.

Students spend years learning why our environment is broken, and what goes wrong. This map asks a different question: where has it actually been fixed, and how? Every pin is a real place, a real problem, and a real solution, documented by our students.

Grounded in planetary boundaries

Every story is tagged to what it actually addressed.

Planetary boundaries mark the limits within which humanity can keep operating safely, covering everything from the climate system to the flow of nitrogen and phosphorus through soil and water. Cross too many of them, and the systems that hold up a livable planet start to break down in ways that are hard to reverse. That's why every story on this map is tagged against the specific boundary it actually helped pull back from the edge, so it's clear not just that something was fixed, but which planetary limit it relieved.
