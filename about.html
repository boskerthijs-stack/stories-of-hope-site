About this project

A map built by students, for the questions students actually ask.

Stories of Hope grew out of fieldwork in the Philippines, East Africa, and the Caribbean, where it became clear that students needed a place to collect not just the problems they were studying, but the solutions they were finding too.

How we ask students to contribute

The map only works if every pin is grounded in something real. A few principles guide every submission.

01

Real places only

Every story is tied to an actual, locatable place, not a hypothetical or composite example.

02

Solutions, not just problems

The map exists to document what changed, the intervention, the policy, the restoration, not only what went wrong.

03

Cite what you can

A short description, in your own words, of the evidence behind the story; no parachute science.

04

Tag it honestly

Mark the planetary boundaries actually addressed, even if it's just one. Precision matters more than breadth.
