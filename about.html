About this project

A map built by students, for the questions students actually ask.

Stories of Hope grew out of fieldwork in the Philippines, East Africa, and the Caribbean, where it became clear that students needed a place to collect not just the problems they were studying, but the solutions they were finding too.

Field photo placeholder, e.g. river monitoring
"Most environmental education teaches collapse. We wanted a place that also teaches repair, in the students' own words, from the field, with evidence."
Programme team, Science for Sustainable Societies, Leiden University
Who built this

Developed by Thijs Bosker and Achim Haeger.

Stories of Hope is developed by Thijs Bosker, Associate Professor in Environmental Science and Programme Director of the BSc Science for Sustainable Societies at Leiden University's Institute of Environmental Sciences, together with Achim Haeger.

Funding

Made possible by the Leiden University Keimfonds.

This project was supported by Leiden University's Keimfonds, which funds early-stage, innovative ideas in education and research across the university.

Curious what this looks like in practice?

Three field sites, followed over years, each with its own story of repair: the Polderlab, agroforestry in Costa Rica, and crocodile conservation in the Philippines.
