About this project

A map built by students, with their stories of hope and change.

Stories of Hope grew out of teaching about environmental issues for over 15 years, where it became clear that students needed a place to collect not just the problems they were studying, but the solutions they were finding too. Stories of change and hope, without looking away from the issues we face.

"Most environmental education teaches about collapse of systems. We wanted a place that also teaches, repair and change, in the students' own words, with stories they care about."
Dr. Thijs Bosker, Associate Professor in Environmental Sciences at Leiden University
Who built this

Developed by Thijs Bosker and Achim Häger.

The two have worked together for seven years, including developing a field course on one of the Dutch Wadden Islands.

Thijs Bosker

Associate Professor, Institute of Environmental Sciences, Leiden University

Thijs is Associate Professor in Environmental Science and Programme Director of the BSc Science for Sustainable Societies. His research centres on plastic pollution, ecotoxicology, and citizen-science-based monitoring, with fieldwork spanning East Africa, the Caribbean, and the Philippines. He built Stories of Hope to give students a place to document not just what's broken, but what's been fixed.

Achim Häger

Assistant Professor, Leiden University College

Achim grew up in the forests of Germany, the grandson of a forest ranger, and later worked in the jungles and coffee plantations of Costa Rica. He now researches dune ecosystems in The Hague with his students and the water company Dunea, studying how dunes store carbon and purify drinking water. "I feel a deep connection with nature," he says, "and I care about researching it as much as I care about protecting it."
